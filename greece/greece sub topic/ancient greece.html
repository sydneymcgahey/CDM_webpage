Black-figure painting was great and all but gave artists limitations bc of the fact that it’s harder to be versatile

            -red figure painting was the solution to this problem, invented by the Andokides painter

Red-figure painting was the complete opposite and used positive space (red) to stand out from negative space (black)

            -painting this way allowed for artists to no longer have to carve the details from the black, but rather draw the details on the red -> greater flexibility

            -this technique helped revolutionize drawing

Discussion questions

Outline the formal and iconographic characteristics of Geometric, Orientalizing, Archaic, Classical, and Hellenistic art.

Geometric art is quite easy to identify, as the name is pretty self-explanatory for the motifs that make this style distinct. The forms that make up both people and general design are made out of rigid shapes that seem to fit a very specific canon, similar to the Egyptian style in its rigidity and consistency.

“Orientalizing” art is where there seems to be an increased amount of contact between Greece and surrounding groups of people, such as the Egyptians, Assyrians, and Mesopotamians. This is evidenced through the maximalist use of space on pieces and depictions of hybrid human/animals, rigid canon that is similar to Egyptian style, and use of animal imagery.

Archaic art is where Greece starts to define its architecture and statuary a little more, still following the general canon of rigid and fairly emotionless. Art during this time was very two dimensional compared to art of the later Classical and Hellenistic periods but is starting to slowly become slightly more realistic with the invention of the “Archaic smile”.

The Classical period is when things start to really change with Greek art and architecture as they start to become more “loose”. This period is dominated by the beginning of the infamous contrapposto pose, which allows for more freedom and variety in sculpture, as seen in pieces such as the Weary Herakles and Aphrodite of Knidos.

Hellenistic art could be seen as the height of Greek art and architecture, as it takes the foundation set by the earlier periods to create pieces that are groundbreaking and thought evoking. This period moves way beyond the contrapposto pose and includes figures in all sorts of positions, doing all sorts of things, and finally there is representation of all sorts of people. The extreme emotion that comes out of this period is also noteworthy, as now the realism is at its height.

 

Describe the importance and innovations of the Kritios Boy and the Doryphoros of Polykleitos as milestones in Greek art and thought.

Prior to the Kritios Boy and Doryphoros of Polykleitos, Greece had a tendency to stick to a canon in their art and the idea of branching out toward individualism wasn’t really on the minds of anyone. It wasn’t until the Kritios Boy came along when all of that changed, due to the simple change in stance found in this statue. Up to this point, artists had never really considered how people stand and so that resulted in the familiar rigidity in Archaic and previous time period art. This catalytic stance is called contrapposto, and involves a simple bend of one leg to create the infamous S curve of the body.

Greek art had been pretty static up until this point, where the Kritios Boy introduces a more dynamic pose. This is quickly seen to be exaggerated in the Doryphoros of Polykleitos. This statue has the same contrapposto pose as that of the Kritios Boy, but the pop of the hip and bend of the leg is more dramatic, resulting in a much more curved body shape. This contrapposto pose, especially the exaggerated version, quickly becomes popular and the Doryphoros of Polykleitos ends up being a highly copied statue.
